## Day22 — System Stabilization Completed

### Summary
Today I finalized the physical structure tuning of the robot and achieved a stable navigation system.  
The robot can now move forward, backward, and turn while maintaining a balanced posture.

This marks the completion of Phase 1 (System Stabilization).

---

### What happened

Previously, the robot had serious stability issues:
- Tilting forward/backward during motion
- Nose touching the ground after stopping
- Oscillation when moving

At first, I tried to fix these issues by tuning inertial parameters,  
but this approach was ineffective.

---

### What I changed

I shifted focus from parameter tuning to **mechanical structure**:

- Added a rear passive support wheel (auxiliary support)
- Tuned the support wheel height with millimeter-level adjustments
- Adjusted base_link height slightly
- Most importantly:
  - Moved both drive wheels slightly forward

---

### Why it worked

The root cause was **poor load distribution**, not control or navigation.

Originally:
- Two-wheel structure + improper support → “seesaw effect”

After adjustment:
- Drive wheels act as the primary support
- Rear support wheel provides secondary stabilization
- Load distribution becomes more balanced

---

### Results

- Stable during forward motion
- Stable during backward motion
- Stable during turning
- No more nose dropping after stopping
- Reduced oscillation
- Navigation becomes smooth and consistent

The system is now physically stable enough for reliable navigation.

---

### System Status

- Gazebo simulation: stable
- SLAM mapping: completed
- AMCL localization: working
- Nav2 navigation: stable

Full navigation pipeline is now:
map → localization → planning → control → movement ✅

---

### Key Insight

- Physical structure matters as much as algorithms
- Do not rely only on parameter tuning to fix structural problems
- Small geometric changes (cm-level) can have large effects
- Debugging order should be:
  structure → physics → parameters → algorithm
