## Day21

### What I did
- Rebuilt the robot structure to improve physical stability in Gazebo
- Identified that instability was mainly caused by poor support structure and sensitive center of mass
- Stopped relying on fine-tuning inertial parameters for balance
- Added a rear passive support wheel (auxiliary wheel) to form a stable three-point support system
- Adjusted base_link height and wheel positions for better ground contact
- Tested robot behavior in simulation (static + small velocity movement)

### Results
- Robot no longer heavily depends on precise inertial tuning to stay balanced
- Stability improved significantly compared to previous two-wheel + caster setup
- Reduced rear dragging and extreme pitching behavior
- System is now closer to a physically reasonable mobile base

### Issues
- Rear support wheel size may still need fine-tuning
- Slight imbalance still exists depending on support wheel contact

### Next Steps
- Fine-tune rear support wheel size and position
- Ensure main wheels remain primary load-bearing components
- Continue testing motion stability before returning to Nav2 parameter tuning
